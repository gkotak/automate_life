Turn Call Centers Into Revenue Drivers: The AI Transformation

Executive Summary

In this episode of the AI Unveil podcast, Swapneel Jain, founder and CEO of observe.ai, discusses how artificial intelligence is fundamentally transforming contact centers from cost centers into revenue drivers. Founded in 2017 as a pre-LLM company, observe.ai has grown to serve 300+ enterprise customers with over 350 employees globally.

The observe.ai Platform

Observe.ai offers three core products that support the hybrid future of contact centers:

  • AI Voice Agents: Autonomous AI agents that handle simpler customer service calls, automating routine interactions
  • Real-time AI Co-pilots: AI assistants that listen to live calls and guide human agents with real-time suggestions and insights
  • Post-interaction Analytics: Comprehensive analysis of both AI and human agent interactions to improve performance and customer experience

Industry Transformation Predictions

Jain predicts that 50% or more of customer service calls will be automated by AI within the next five years. However, he emphasizes this won't eliminate human agents entirely - instead, it will create a hybrid model where AI handles knowledge-based queries while humans focus on complex, sensitive, or empathy-requiring interactions.

The BPO Industry Impact

Traditional Business Process Outsourcing (BPO) companies face significant challenges as AI automation reduces demand for human agents. Unlike other industries where "service-as-software" models can work, contact centers are controlled by enterprises who dictate technology stacks, making it difficult for BPOs to transform independently.

Revenue Center Transformation

Companies are shifting from viewing contact centers as cost centers to revenue drivers. By automating simple queries with AI, businesses can redirect human agents to higher-value activities like cross-selling, upselling, and customer acquisition - fundamentally changing the economics of customer service.

Technology and Implementation

Observe.ai follows a "foundation model first" approach, starting with third-party LLMs for rapid prototyping and product-market fit, then optimizing with smaller, domain-specific models based on business needs. The company has developed comprehensive testing frameworks using AI agents to evaluate other AI agents.

Global Operations and Leadership

With 200 employees in India and the remainder in the US, observe.ai has successfully built a distributed team while maintaining customer centricity through regular customer visits, shared win stories, and transparent communication across time zones.

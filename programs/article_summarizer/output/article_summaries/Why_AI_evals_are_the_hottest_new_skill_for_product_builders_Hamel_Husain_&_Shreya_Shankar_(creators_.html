Summary

AI evaluations (evals) have emerged as the most critical new skill for product builders in the age of AI applications. Hamel Husain and Shreya Shankar, who teach the world's most popular AI evals course, demonstrate that evals are fundamentally about systematic measurement and improvement of AI applications through data analytics. Rather than being scary or unapproachable, evals provide the highest ROI activity for AI product development by giving teams concrete feedback signals to iterate against.

The core methodology involves a structured process starting with error analysis, where teams manually review real user traces to identify failure patterns through 'open coding' - writing detailed notes about what goes wrong. This data is then synthesized using 'axial coding' to categorize errors into actionable failure modes. The most critical insight is that teams must look at actual user data first, rather than jumping straight into writing tests or relying on generic evaluation tools.

The process culminates in building targeted 'LLM-as-judge' evaluators for the most persistent failure modes that can't be easily fixed with simple prompt changes. These binary pass/fail evaluators become living product requirements documents that can run continuously in production, providing ongoing quality monitoring. The entire system transforms chaotic user interactions into systematic product improvements, typically requiring just 3-4 days of initial setup and 30 minutes weekly maintenance.

Despite some controversy in the AI community about whether evals are necessary, with some teams claiming to rely purely on 'vibes,' the reality is that all successful AI products use some form of systematic evaluation - whether through formal evals, rigorous dogfooding, or data analysis. The key is moving beyond subjective 'vibe checks' to systematic, data-driven approaches that scale as products grow in complexity.

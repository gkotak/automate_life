Turn Call Centers Into Revenue Drivers: AI-Powered Transformation

In this comprehensive interview, Swapnil Jain, founder and CEO of Observe AI, discusses how artificial intelligence is fundamentally transforming contact centers from cost centers into revenue-generating engines. Founded in 2017 as a pre-LLM company, Observe AI has grown to serve over 300 enterprise customers including major banks and telecom companies.

The Contact Center Revolution

Observe AI operates at the intersection of human and artificial intelligence in customer service. The company provides three core solutions: AI agents that can automate simple customer interactions, real-time AI co-pilots that assist human agents during complex calls, and post-interaction analytics that mine conversation data for insights and continuous improvement.

Jain predicts that 50% or more of customer service calls will be automated by AI within the next five years, though enterprise adoption may be slower due to legacy infrastructure challenges. The key distinction lies between knowledge-based queries (easily automated) and workflow-based queries requiring complex integrations and policy decisions.

Industry Impact and Transformation

The BPO industry faces significant disruption as AI agents reduce demand for human agents. Companies like T-Mobile have publicly announced partnerships with OpenAI specifically to reduce agent headcount. However, Jain envisions a hybrid future where AI handles routine queries while human agents focus on complex, sensitive, or empathy-requiring interactions.

Interestingly, the cost reduction from AI automation is enabling companies to transform contact centers into revenue centers. By automating simple queries, businesses can redeploy human agents for cross-selling, upselling, and customer acquisition activities that directly generate revenue.

Technology and Innovation

Observe AI employs both proprietary small language models (SLMs) and partnerships with major LLM providers. Their approach emphasizes rapid iteration through customer feedback - "selling then building" rather than perfecting products in isolation. The company has developed sophisticated evaluation frameworks using AI agents to test other AI agents, creating multi-layered quality assurance systems.

The shift toward agentic AI represents more than just automation - it's transforming human-computer interfaces from click-based UIs to conversational, reasoning-based interactions across all software applications.

Global Operations and Scaling

With approximately 200 employees in India handling engineering, ML, and product development, Observe AI demonstrates how distributed teams can successfully serve enterprise customers. The company maintains customer proximity through regular visits, shared communication channels, and systematic knowledge transfer processes.

Jain emphasizes the evolution required of CEOs as companies scale, noting his transformation toward greater intensity and ambition, driven by the realization that in venture-backed companies, there are only two outcomes: massive success or failure - making "good enough" insufficient.

Turn Call Centers Into Revenue Drivers: AI-Powered Transformation

In this comprehensive interview, Swapnil Jain, founder and CEO of Observe AI, discusses how artificial intelligence is revolutionizing contact centers and transforming them from cost centers into revenue-generating assets. Founded in 2017 as a "pre-LLM company," Observe AI has grown to serve 300+ enterprise customers with a team of 350+ employees globally.

The Contact Center AI Revolution

Observe AI operates as a contact center AI platform serving large enterprises including banks and telecom companies. The company offers three core products: AI agents for call automation, real-time AI co-pilots for human agents, and post-interaction analytics. Their approach addresses the full spectrum of customer service interactions, from simple knowledge-based queries to complex workflow-driven tasks.

Market Transformation and BPO Impact

Jain predicts that 50%+ of customer service calls will be automated within five years, with significant implications for the BPO industry. Traditional business process outsourcing companies face disruption as enterprises like T-Mobile invest heavily in AI solutions to reduce agent dependencies. However, this creates opportunities for companies that can successfully transition from human-centric to AI-first service models.

Technical Innovation and Testing

The company employs a sophisticated testing framework using AI agents to test other AI agents, creating an interconnected system of evaluation and improvement. They start with foundation models for rapid iteration and product-market fit validation, then optimize based on specific use cases, security requirements, and cost considerations. This approach allows for faster deployment while maintaining quality and reliability.

Strategic Business Model Evolution

Rather than viewing AI as a threat to existing revenue streams, Observe AI embraces self-disruption. They're willing to cannibalize their human-seat-based revenue to capture AI agent revenue, positioning themselves as the disruptor rather than the disrupted. This strategic pivot enables customers to transform contact centers from cost centers into revenue drivers through improved cross-selling, upselling, and customer acquisition capabilities.

Global Operations and Customer-Centric Development

With 200+ employees in India and strong US market presence, the company demonstrates how distributed teams can maintain customer centricity through structured processes, regular customer exposure, and transparent communication. Their "sell then build" philosophy ensures customer needs drive product development, accelerating innovation cycles and market responsiveness.

💡 Key Insights

  • 50%+ of customer service calls will be automated by AI within 5 years, fundamentally reshaping the contact center industry
  • Traditional BPO companies face existential threat as enterprises move from human-agent models to AI-first solutions, requiring complete business model transformation
  • Contact centers are evolving from cost centers to revenue drivers, enabling better cross-selling, upselling, and customer acquisition through AI automation of simple tasks
  • "Service as Software" model doesn't apply to contact centers because enterprises maintain control over policies, processes, and technology stack, limiting outsourcer transformation ability
  • AI testing requires AI - Observe AI uses interconnected AI agents to test, evaluate, and improve other AI agents, creating self-improving systems
  • Voice remains dominant (75% of interactions) over chat (20%) due to consumer preference, but cost advantages of AI may reverse the trend toward chat adoption
  • Customer-led product development through "sell then build" philosophy accelerates innovation and ensures market fit over internal perfectionism
  • Self-disruption strategy is essential - companies must cannibalize their existing revenue streams to capture AI transformation opportunities before competitors do

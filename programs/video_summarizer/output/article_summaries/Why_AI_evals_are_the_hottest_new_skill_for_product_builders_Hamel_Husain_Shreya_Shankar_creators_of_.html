üéØ What Are AI Evals? ‚è∞ 5:30

Evals are systematic methods for measuring and improving AI applications. At their core, they're data analytics applied to LLM applications - providing structured ways to understand what's happening and iterate with confidence. Unlike traditional software where you can predict behavior, LLMs are stochastic systems with much larger surface areas for potential issues.

üîç The Error Analysis Process ‚è∞ 12:00

The foundation of effective evals is manual error analysis - looking at real user traces and writing notes about what's going wrong. This process involves:

  • Open Coding: Writing informal notes about the first problem you see in each trace
  • Theoretical Saturation: Continuing until you stop discovering new types of problems (typically 100+ traces)
  • Axial Coding: Using AI to categorize and synthesize your notes into actionable failure modes
  • Prioritization: Counting and ranking issues to focus on the most impactful problems

üèóÔ∏è Live Example: Real Estate Assistant ‚è∞ 11:00

Hamel demonstrates the process using real data from Nurture Boss, an AI assistant for property managers. The analysis reveals various failure modes:

  • Poor handoff decisions when customers need human assistance
  • Conversational flow issues with text messaging
  • Hallucinating features that don't exist (like virtual tours)
  • Formatting errors and protocol violations

‚öñÔ∏è The Benevolent Dictator Approach ‚è∞ 25:40

To avoid committee paralysis, appoint one domain expert as the "benevolent dictator" for error analysis. This person should have the necessary context to judge whether AI responses make sense for the business case. Often this is the product manager, but it should always be someone with domain expertise.

ü§ñ LLM as Judge Evaluators ‚è∞ 48:10

After identifying failure modes through manual analysis, you can build automated evaluators:

  • Code-based evals: Simple Python functions for straightforward checks (formatting, length, etc.)
  • LLM as judge: Using another LLM to evaluate complex, subjective criteria
  • Binary scoring: Always use pass/fail rather than numerical scales
  • Validation required: Test your judge against human labels before deploying

üí≠ Addressing the Evals Debate ‚è∞ 71:00

The controversy around evals often stems from misconceptions and bad experiences. People get burned by poorly implemented LLM judges or have narrow definitions of what evals encompass. The reality is that successful AI products are built on some form of systematic evaluation - even if teams don't explicitly call it "evals."

üí° Getting Started: Tips and Best Practices ‚è∞ 87:00

  • Don't be scared of looking at your data - it's the highest ROI activity
  • Use AI to help organize your thoughts and categorize findings
  • Build custom tools to make data review as frictionless as possible
  • Focus on 4-7 key evaluators rather than trying to test everything
  • Expect about a week of upfront work, then 30 minutes weekly maintenance

Why AI Evals Are the Hottest New Skill for Product Builders

This comprehensive discussion with Hamel Husain and Shreya Shankar, creators of the #1 AI evals course on Maven, explores why evaluations have become the most critical skill for AI product builders and provides a deep dive into practical implementation strategies.

🎯 The Rise of Evals as Essential Skills ⏰ 0:00

AI evaluations have emerged from obscurity to become one of the most necessary skills for product builders. Both Anthropic and OpenAI's Chief Product Officers have identified evals as the most important new skill, representing the highest ROI activity for building great AI products. The speakers have taught over 2,000 PMs and engineers across 500+ companies, including major AI labs.

🔍 Understanding What Evals Actually Are ⏰ 5:51

At its core, evals are a systematic way to measure and improve AI applications through data analytics. Rather than being scary or unapproachable, evals function as structured analysis of LLM application performance, enabling teams to create metrics, measure outcomes, and iterate for improvement. The process involves examining real user interactions and identifying patterns in AI system behavior.

⚡ The Addictive Nature and Immediate Value ⏰ 0:07

Teams quickly become addicted to the eval process due to its immediate learning benefits. Most products only need to go through the comprehensive eval process once, then build upon that foundation. The goal isn't perfection in evaluation but actionable product improvement through systematic analysis.

🚫 Common Misconceptions and Pitfalls ⏰ 0:39

The biggest misconception is believing AI can evaluate itself effectively. Many teams attempt to automate the evaluation process entirely, but this approach consistently fails. Other teams get bogged down in committee-based decision making, making the process unnecessarily expensive and slow.

👑 The Benevolent Dictator Approach ⏰ 0:48

Effective eval teams use a "benevolent dictator" model where one trusted person with domain expertise (often the product manager) makes decisions during the open coding process. This prevents committees from slowing down progress while ensuring quality outcomes through concentrated expertise.

📊 Practical Implementation Strategy

The conversation provides detailed walkthroughs of developing effective evals, addressing major misconceptions, and sharing battle-tested best practices. The speakers emphasize starting with manual error analysis before attempting any automation, using real user traces to understand system performance, and maintaining focus on actionable improvements rather than perfect measurement.

🎓 Educational Impact and Industry Adoption

The Maven course has become the definitive training for eval skills, with widespread adoption across major AI companies. The methodology has shifted evals from an obscure, mysterious subject to an essential competency for AI product builders, with the speakers playing a major role in this transformation.

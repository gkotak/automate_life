Summary

Obsidian + AI: How to Do It The Right Way

🤔 The Philosophy: Protecting Your Sacred Thinking Space ⏰ 0:00

Nick Milo begins by addressing the fundamental tension between AI and Obsidian's core value proposition. At 0:55, he emphasizes that "Obsidian to me has always represented a sacred thinking space, an idea verse for my own thoughts." The concern is that AI encourages other people's thoughts to enter this sacred space, potentially causing your idea verse to lose what made it come alive in the first place - your voice.

🛡️ The IDI Framework: Defense Strategy ⏰ 1:42

Milo introduces his personal framework for responsible AI interaction: **Imagine, Discern, Integrate**. At 1:54, he breaks down each step: - **Imagine**: AI opens up possibilities and helps us think about what's even possible - **Discern**: Evaluate AI output critically - even if something is untrue, it can still be useful - **Integrate**: Connect AI-assisted work with the rest of your life and opportunities

⚖️ The Barbell Approach: Defense vs Offense ⏰ 2:33

**Defensive Side - Minimizing Downsides:** - Avoid overgeneration: Don't let AI-generated text overtake your own writing - Privacy considerations: Choose your position on the spectrum from local (safest) to cloud-based AI like ChatGPT (riskiest but cutting-edge) - Middle ground options exist with cloud-based but non-training models **Offensive Side - Maximizing Benefits:** - Use AI to reflect themes and highlights from recent thinking - Leverage AI as "the tip of the spear for deep research" - Most importantly at 3:47: "create a dedicated AI zone" - have a clear place for AI-generated content to create "good friction" between AI output and your personal idea verse

🔧 The Tool: Claude Code Integration ⏰ 4:34

Milo's recommended solution is Claude Code from Anthropic, which differs from regular Claude by being able to "chain together multiple independent steps and execute on tasks for you based on files and folders on your computer." At 4:56, he explains that "Claude Code can actually analyze your notes on your computer, edit and restructure them." Key advantages: - Works with Obsidian's file-and-folder structure - Can analyze, edit, and restructure notes automatically - Add information across hundreds of notes - Spin up sub-agents for web research - At 5:18: "everything stays in simple markdown files that you own"

💡 Practical Implementation ⏰ 5:36

Milo demonstrates the workflow by opening terminal, launching Claude Code, and directing it to analyze his "idea verse" - specifically asking it to "analyze all the notes I've written about in the past 45 days." The system automatically saves the analysis to a separate AI-dedicated Obsidian vault, maintaining clear separation between AI-generated content and personal notes. This maintains the principle of keeping AI content in its own dedicated zone while still leveraging its analytical power for reflection and insight generation.

Why AI Evals Are the Hottest New Skill for Product Builders

This podcast episode features Hamel Husain and Shreya Shankar, creators of the #1 AI evaluation course, discussing why AI evaluations have become essential for product builders working with AI systems.

What Are AI Evaluations?

AI evaluations (evals) are systematic processes for testing and improving AI product performance. Unlike traditional software testing, AI evals involve manually reviewing user interactions to identify patterns, failure modes, and areas for improvement. The hosts emphasize that evals serve as "living product requirements documents" for AI products.

The Critical Importance of Evals

The discussion highlights several key reasons why evals are crucial:

  • Proactive Problem Detection: Help uncover upstream issues before they become systemic problems
  • Performance Understanding: Essential for comprehending AI system limitations and capabilities
  • User Experience Optimization: Enable teams to understand real user interactions and pain points
  • Quality Assurance: Provide a structured approach to maintaining and improving AI system quality

Implementation Framework

The experts outline a practical approach to implementing AI evaluations:

  1. Manual Error Analysis: Always begin with hands-on review of system failures
  2. Open Coding: Categorize and understand error patterns systematically
  3. Axial Coding: Synthesize findings to develop deeper insights
  4. AI Judge Testing: Validate automated evaluation systems against human judgment

Practical Considerations

The conversation addresses real-world implementation challenges and solutions. After initial setup, teams typically spend about 30 minutes per week maintaining their evaluation systems. The focus should be on understanding real user traces rather than relying solely on synthetic testing scenarios.

Common Pitfalls and Misconceptions

The hosts debunk several common misconceptions about AI evaluations. They emphasize that LLMs cannot fully replace human analysis in initial error review stages, and that dogfooding (internal testing) alone is insufficient for comprehensive AI product testing.

This episode provides actionable guidance for product managers, AI developers, and engineers looking to systematically improve their AI-powered products through structured evaluation processes.

Turn Call Centers into Revenue Drivers: The AI Transformation

In this comprehensive interview, Swapnil Jan, founder and CEO of Observe AI, discusses how artificial intelligence is fundamentally transforming contact centers from cost centers into revenue drivers. The conversation reveals insights into the future of customer service automation and the strategic opportunities emerging in the AI-first contact center landscape.

The Observe AI Story

Founded in 2017 as a "pre-LLM company," Observe AI began when speech recognition technology was improving through deep learning, even before the current generative AI boom. Jan's aha moment came not from technology, but from spending six months in Manila's call centers, observing the daily challenges faced by human agents and supervisors. This customer-centric approach led to building AI solutions that address real operational pain points.

Three-Pillar Product Strategy

Observe AI operates on a hybrid vision where AI agents handle simple queries while human agents manage complex, sensitive conversations. Their platform consists of three key products:

  • AI Agents: Autonomous voice and chat agents that automate simpler customer service interactions, reducing the need for human intervention on routine queries
  • Real-time AI Co-pilots: AI assistants that listen to live conversations between customers and human agents, providing real-time guidance, transcription, and recommendations to improve resolution quality
  • Post-interaction Analytics: Comprehensive analysis of both AI and human interactions to understand performance, quality, and customer experience, enabling continuous improvement

Industry Transformation and Revenue Opportunities

The discussion reveals a paradigm shift where contact centers are evolving from transactional cost centers to strategic revenue generators. Jan shares examples of customers redesigning their products to increase customer touchpoints with support teams, using AI to handle volume while human agents focus on cross-selling, upselling, and retention.

This transformation is enabled by AI reducing the cost differential between voice and chat interactions. Historically, chat was promoted despite poor customer preference due to cost advantages (chat agents could handle multiple conversations simultaneously). With AI agents, voice interactions become cost-effective, aligning with customer preferences.

The BPO Industry Disruption

Traditional Business Process Outsourcing (BPO) companies face significant challenges as enterprises reduce their reliance on human agents. Jan predicts major difficulties for BPOs whose primary business model is "selling human agents." Unlike other service industries that can transform through "services as software" models, contact centers present unique constraints because enterprises dictate policies, procedures, and technology stacks, limiting outsourcers' ability to independently innovate.

Technical Innovation and Quality Assurance

The company addresses AI quality and non-deterministic behavior through sophisticated testing frameworks. They create AI agents that test other AI agents, simulating diverse customer personas with different accents, backgrounds, and scenarios. This "agents testing agents" approach enables rapid iteration and quality improvement while maintaining the conversational flexibility that makes modern AI agents superior to traditional rule-based chatbots.

Global Operations and Customer Centricity

With 350 employees and 200+ in their India engineering center, Observe AI demonstrates how global teams can maintain customer focus despite geographical challenges. They've implemented processes including regular customer visits, shared win stories, and frequent all-hands meetings to ensure engineering teams understand customer needs and market dynamics.

Future Outlook

Jan envisions 50%+ automation of contact center interactions within five years, though enterprise adoption timelines vary due to legacy system constraints. The increasing digitization of business-to-consumer interactions creates growing demand for contact centers as the primary human interaction channel, potentially offsetting job displacement through increased strategic utilization of human agents for complex, high-value interactions.
